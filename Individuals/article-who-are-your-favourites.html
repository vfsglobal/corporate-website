• England

      The 2019 World Cup will be the fifth time that England have staged the World Cup. Having featured in every World Cup since the tournament was established in 1975 but have still to win it, England will look to make the most of this tournament. With their current form, England is surely one of the favourites to lift the trophy. However, they’ll have to be at their best from the start as they take on South Africa in the opener followed by Pakistan.

    • Australia

      The defending champions and five-time winners, Australia is the most successful country in the tournament’s history. In 2015, they beat New Zealand by seven wickets in the final. However, they have struggled for form since the team was caught up in a ball-tampering scandal that rocked the game last year. Apart from fixing their bowling, Australia also needs to sort out their middle order. Australia’s chances have improved after their ODI and T20 win over India. They face Afghanistan to open the World Cup.

    • India

      Under the leadership of Virat Kohli, India will enter the World Cup as favourites alongside England. Most players of the Indian team are in great form and they play well in England. Former Indian captain Sourav Ganguly also described India as favourites to lift the World Cup, saying the team is in best possible shape to win the quadrennial event which gets underway at England on May 30. India will begin its campaign at the World Cup with a clash against South Africa on June 5 at Southampton.

    • New Zealand

      The Kiwis has never won the World Cup, but they have come close to winning the prestigious trophy, having reached the semifinals on seven occasions and even made it to the final in the 2015 edition, only to lose out to Australia. However, the current side has some world class players who can turn any game in their favour. While they’ve long been termed as dark horses (even Sachin said that New Zealand would be the dark horse this year), this time they might turn out to be serious contenders to lift the trophy.

    • Added Choice and Convenience for UK Visa Customers

      A range of optional services aimed at providing a stress-free application experience can now be opted for online on www.gov.uk, at the application stage. Services include:

      • Convenience: Customers can opt for the ‘Walk-in without appointment’ service (for customers seeking to submit applications urgently), or ‘Keep My Passport While Applying’ (enabling customers to apply for other visas during the processing of UK visa) at the application stage itself
      • Comfort: Personalised services at the Premium Lounge
      • Speed: Super Priority Service and Priority Service for fast-track turnaround times
      • Peace of mind: Document-scanning assistance for customers opting for self-upload

      • Opt for ‘Check and Verify’ Service for Greater Confidence in Your Application

      VFS Global, in association with UK Visa and Immigration (UKVI), has also launched a new Check and Verify service for UK visa applicants across India. Using this service, customers can opt to run their documentation past trained VFS Global professionals for independent authentication, and have the verification report sent directly to UKVI. This service provides customers with a higher degree of assurance about the documents / information submitted, while giving greater confidence to UKVI about the genuineness of the document. In view of rising UK visa application volumes, the service will also strengthen the integrity and security of the visa process, by mitigating the influence of touts and risks of fraudulent applications.

      How Check and Verify Works:

      For customers opting for the Check and Verify service*, VFS Global staff will undertake the first level of scrutiny on the information / documents submitted – through designated checks on bank statements, income tax returns and employment. VFS Global will provide an independent validation of the documents and submit an official verification report directly to UK Visas & Immigration. The service can be purchased online during the online visa application process or at the Visa Application Centre itself.


    • *Availing the Check & Verify Service does not guarantee a visa. The decision of your visa and processing time is at the sole discretion of UKVI.
