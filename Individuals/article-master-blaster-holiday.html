• How should one begin writing about Sachin Tendulkar - the man who very nearly defines and embodies the love for the game, Cricket, in India and abroad. Besides his game, there are legends that his friends, fans and teammates narrate about his humility and how absolutely ‘normal’ he is. It so turns out that the Master Blaster is just like any other regular guy who has an equal fondness for home-cooked food as well as gourmet meals. He loves impromptu singing sessions and driving around the city with his friends, especially on weekends!

      Here are a few things about the way Sachin Tendulkar travels that will convince you that there is much more to him than being a cricketer.

    • Your idea of a perfect holiday:

      Thanks to my career, travelling has always been a part of the game for me and is as close to my heart as cricket. Of late, I’ve been visiting places where cricket has not taken me. A perfect holiday for me would be a break when I can relax with my family and friends, and of course, holidays are not perfect without good food. After retirement, holidays are more about luxury getaways; I enjoy mountains and cold climates.

    • What are your favourite destinations?

      England has always been one of my favourite family holiday destinations, especially during the summer season. We do a lot of family activities there that’s otherwise difficult in India; little joys of life like going to the park with my kids. In India, it is Mussoorie - I love the views and the peace and calm. I was in Mussoorie for a snowed-out winter some years ago and it was magical.

    • What’s your favourite food destination and cuisine?

      Apart from home-cooked food, I love Japanese cuisine. Japan is definitely on my wish list; I haven’t been there yet.

    • What have been your favourite grounds to play - in India and abroad?

      Oh, Lords, without a doubt! And in India, Wankhede and Eden Gardens.

    • Any memorable travel experiences?

      Several of them! But just randomly, I was watching Harry Potter on TV the other day. And it made me recollect how in 2007, during the Lord’s test, there was a very familiar looking face that was standing in a queue for my autograph. I asked him his name and he said, “Daniel” and then I was told it was Daniel Radcliffe.

      There are some unhappy or inconvenient memories as well. Like when I was returning to Mumbai from Shanghai and my flight got delayed by 2 hours due to which I missed my connecting flight. I had to fly down to Bangalore instead and ended up spending the night at the airport there. It was not a happy travel experience for sure!

    • What would you like to say to a first-time traveller?

      I think in today’s day and age, the first time traveller would perhaps be a small baby! (laughs) But if I have to say something, I would say, be adventurous only when you have family or friends with you. Stay safe. That, and also meet as many local people as possible. They will tell you more about a place than anything else ever can.

    • What are some things that you never travel without?

      Pictures of my parents, my kids …and my iPod, iPhone and chapstick.

    • What are some destinations that you would like to travel to?

      Other than Japan... the Poles, maybe. And yes, Russia too. In India, I would like to explore the Himalayas and how different they are in different parts of India.

    Sachin picks his holiday preferences!


    Sun or snow?

    Snow, with bright sunshine. Like in the Alps.

    Mountains or beaches?

    Mountains!

    Relaxation or adventure?

    A mix of both. Relaxation mostly when I am with family and adventure with friends.

    Countryside or city lights?

    Countryside in England and Switzerland. City lights in my city, Mumbai.

    Wildlife or night life?

    Some would choose wild nightlife, but I am over that now! (laughs) But to answer your question, at the moment, wildlife!

    Shopping: High street, Couture or Bargains

    My wife shops for me. I am so bad at shopping!
