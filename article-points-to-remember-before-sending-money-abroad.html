• If you have family, friends or a business based in another country you may need to send money from time to time. Such a requirement may also come up if you are moving overseas. Here are some of the key things to know for international services.

      What details do you need?

      Before you send the money it's important that you have the following details in hand about the person (beneficiary) you are sending money to and the payment you are wanting to make:

      • Your account details
      • Beneficiary's full name and address
      • Beneficiary's bank code – normally the BIC (Bank Identifier Code)
      • Beneficiary’s IBAN (International Bank Account number) or account number. An IBAN is a series of letters and numbers that help identify accounts held anywhere in the world, some beneficiaries may hold just an account number depending on their bank
      • Country of the beneficiary’s bank
      • Amount of the payment

    • How much can you send?

      This will vary depending on your bank and your daily limit. It is best to gather this information from your bank.

    • What exchange rate is used?

      The exchange rate will change depending on the day and time your payment is made. It will also depend on the bank or company you use to send the money. Before completing the transfer your bank should be able to tell you about the exchange rate used.

    • How much does it cost to transfer

      This will depend on your bank and the beneficiary’s bank
